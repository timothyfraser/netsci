When to book vs. when to ask the bot

The Course Companion (bubble in the bottom-right) and the FAQ catch most evening blockers. Office hours are best reserved for things humans handle better than bots:

Project data

"Is this dataset OK for the project?" "How do I get a network out of this CSV?" Bring a sample.

Stuck conceptually

"I've read the page three times and I still don't see why this matters." Five minutes of conversation usually fixes that.

Picking labs

"Which three of the eleven case studies should I do for the project?" Best answered by talking through your interests.

Accommodations or scheduling

SYSEN 5940 overlap, religious observance, SDS accommodations — short live conversation, fewer email rounds.

Before you arrive

Two minutes of prep saves ten minutes of the meeting:

— Have your code open if it's a code question.
— Have the error message copied if there is one.
— Have your sketchpad ready if it's conceptual.
— Have a one-sentence version of the question ready to lead with — it makes the rest of the conversation faster.
