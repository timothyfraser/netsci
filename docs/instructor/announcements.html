Goes out every Monday around 9 AM as the week's main "we're rolling" message.

This week we're working on the next two case studies — the labs are
already on the site, the sketchpad prompts are open, and the Monday
{{duedate}} submission deadline is the usual: sketches, both LC

If you're picking your project case study this week, lock it in by

🌀 Mid-week nudge

Thursdays. A short check-in to surface the most common stuck spot of the week.

Halfway through Week {{week}}.

By now you should have your project network in hand and a draft
answer to the question. If you're still hunting for a dataset, ping
me — I'd rather manufacture one for you than have you spend Friday
on Kaggle.

The most common stuck spot at this point in the week is operationalization
— "what's a node and what's an edge in MY data." If that's where you
are, the Build a Network case study and Sketch 01 are the unstuck
patterns.

— Tim

⚖ AI-policy reminder

Send once early in the term, then again before the first project report is due.

Quick reminder on the AI policy as you start writing reports.

AI is welcome for syntax and debugging — use it. AI is NOT for
writing your prose, answering Learning Checks, or filling in
reflection prompts. The course is about your thinking; reports are
how we see your thinking.

The Course Companion (the bubble bottom-right of any page) is the
recommended AI for this course. It's grounded in our own materials
and won't write your report for you, by design.

Full policy: https://timothyfraser.com/netsci/help/ai-policy.html

— Tim
